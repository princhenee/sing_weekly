NUMFabric Fast and Flexible Bandwidth Allocation in Datacenters

Summary:

This work proposes NUMFabric, a novel transport design that provides
flexible and fast bandwidth allocation control. NUMFabric enables
operators to flexibly optimize resource allocation in order to meet
service level objectives. By decoupling network utilization from resource
allocation via explicit packet scheduling in the network, NUMFabric
shows how sophisticated packet scheduling can help implement diverse
policies at datacenter scales.

However, it is still an open question as to how higher
layer system can take advantage of NUMFabricâ€™s capabilities
to optimize application layer objectives and this is
the focus of our current work. We are extending NUMFabric
to more general definitions of flows such as coflows,
VM-level aggregates and tenant-level aggregates.

OpenNF Enabling Innovation in Network Function Control

Summary:

The presentation consists of two parts, where the first part
gives a brief high-level introduction to Software-defined Networks
, OpenFlow and Network Function Virtualization. The following
part is a focus on paper introdcution.

This paper solves the race conditon occurred during internal
states migrations. It introduces a new network state layer
in the Controller and an event-driven strategy to guarantee
the consistency and order of states movement.

How to read a paper

  • To fully understand a paper, reader should normally answer the
    following questions after reading:

    • What problem is the paper solving and why is it important?
    • What is the main idea of the paper?
    • How does the paper differ from previous work?
    • Are there any flaws in the paper? How would you improve the paper or build on it in future work?
